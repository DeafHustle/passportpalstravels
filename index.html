Flights to Zanzibar & Tanzania

Zanzibar's Abeid Amani Karume International Airport (ZNZ) welcomes over 1,300 flights monthly from 34 airports worldwide, served by 31 airlines. While there are no direct flights from the US, the most efficient route from New York connects through Nairobi with Kenya Airways, taking approximately 16 hours.

From the UK, popular connecting hubs include Istanbul (Turkish Airlines), Doha (Qatar Airways), Nairobi (Kenya Airways), and Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines). The cheapest month to fly from London is typically May, while July tends to be the most expensive.

For travelers heading to the northern safari circuit — the Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, or Mount Kilimanjaro — Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) is your best option. Dar es Salaam's Julius Nyerere International Airport (DAR) serves as Tanzania's main hub with frequent domestic connections to Zanzibar (20-minute flights departing every 1–2 hours).

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there direct flights from the US to Zanzibar?

No, there are currently no direct flights from the US to Zanzibar. The shortest route is from New York (JFK) via Nairobi with Kenya Airways, taking about 16 hours. Other popular routing options go through Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul.

What's the cheapest time to fly to Zanzibar?

May and early June tend to offer the lowest fares, as this falls during Tanzania's long rainy season. July and August are peak season with the highest prices. February is also a good value month for UK departures.

Do I need a visa for Tanzania?

Most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at any Tanzanian airport for approximately $50 USD. You can also apply for an e-visa in advance through the Tanzanian immigration website.

Which airlines fly to Zanzibar?

Major airlines serving Zanzibar include Turkish Airlines, Qatar Airways, Kenya Airways, Ethiopian Airlines, Emirates, KLM, Air France, and Air Tanzania. Some seasonal direct routes operate from Amsterdam and Istanbul.

How do I get from Dar es Salaam to Zanzibar?

You can take a short 20-minute domestic flight (departures every 1–2 hours from around 7:30am to 6pm) or the Azam Marine ferry, which takes about 2 hours.
